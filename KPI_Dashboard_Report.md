KPI Dashboard Report: SLA Target Setting by Experience Level

#Executive Summary
I developed an interactive KPI dashboard to analyze customer support ticket resolution performance across agent experience levels. The analysis of 1,289 tickets spanning from July 2016 to present reveals that junior agents (0-2 years experience) resolve tickets fastest at 33.90 hours average, followed closely by mid-level agents at 36.62 hours and senior agents at 36.59 hours. This insight enables data-driven SLA target-setting that accounts for experience level while identifying performance benchmarks for coaching and performance management.

#Project Objective
Setting realistic SLA (Service Level Agreement) targets is critical for customer support operations. Without experience-level-adjusted targets, organizations risk creating unattainable goals for junior staff or enabling complacency among senior agents. This dashboard provides a real-time tool for analyzing resolution performance across experience levels, enabling management to set fair, achievable SLAs while identifying outliers and improvement opportunities.

#Key Findings
Analysis of 1,289 support tickets across all categories and priorities reveals clear performance patterns by experience level. Junior agents resolved tickets in an average of 33.90 hours, the fastest among all groups. Mid-level agents averaged 36.62 hours—slightly slower despite having more experience. Senior agents averaged 36.59 hours, nearly identical to mid-level performance. This counterintuitive pattern suggests that experience level alone does not determine speed; ticket complexity, category, and priority may be stronger predictors of resolution time.
Response time also shows minimal variation: junior agents responded in 126.95 minutes on average, while mid-level agents averaged 119.08 minutes and seniors 123.98 minutes. The overall team average resolution time is 36.14 hours with a first-response average of 123.20 minutes. Ticket volume by experience level reflects team composition, with 728 tickets handled by senior agents, 340 by mid-level agents, and 221 by junior agents.

Dashboard Features
The interactive dashboard includes filters for issue category, experience level, date range, and reopened ticket status, allowing managers to explore performance across different dimensions. The primary visualization displays average resolution time by experience level as a bar chart, making performance comparison immediate and actionable. Supporting metrics include ticket volume by experience level, first-response time benchmarks, and overall SLA distribution. A downloadable CSV export enables further analysis in spreadsheet tools.

#SLA Recommendations
Based on the performance data, the following SLA targets are recommended:
Junior Agents (0-2 years experience): 36 hours target. Junior agents demonstrate the fastest resolution times at 33.90 hours, suggesting they are well-suited to straightforward tickets. Setting a 36-hour SLA allows room for complex cases while maintaining accountability to their demonstrated capability.

Mid-Level Agents (3-5 years experience): 38 hours target. Mid-level agents averaged 36.62 hours, slightly above junior performance. A 38-hour SLA accounts for their slightly longer resolution time while maintaining a reasonable stretch goal.
Senior Agents (5+ years experience): 38 hours target. Despite greater experience, senior agents averaged 36.59 hours—essentially equivalent to mid-level performance. A 38-hour SLA sets consistent expectations across experience levels while recognizing that seniority may bring different value propositions (mentoring, complex case handling) beyond pure speed.

#How to Use the Dashboard
Managers can leverage this dashboard to monitor ongoing performance and adjust SLA targets as needed. First, set your date range using the date filters to analyze current performance or historical trends. Next, select specific issue categories or experience levels to isolate performance in key areas. The "Avg Resolution Time by Experience Level" chart provides the primary insight for SLA target-setting. Use the "Ticket Count by Experience Level" chart to understand workload distribution and identify resource allocation opportunities. Download filtered data for deeper analysis or reporting to leadership.

#Next Steps
Monitor actual performance against the recommended SLA targets over the next quarter. If junior agents consistently exceed their 36-hour target, investigate whether ticket routing is optimized or if training needs exist. For mid-level and senior agents, track whether the 38-hour target drives performance improvements or requires adjustment. Use the dashboard's category and priority filters to identify which ticket types consistently miss targets—this may indicate need for specialized training or process improvements. Re-run this analysis quarterly to validate SLA targets and identify performance trends over time.
